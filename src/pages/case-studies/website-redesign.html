Product

Voordeeltuitjes is an online travel agency (OTA) offering curated hotel stay packages across the Netherlands and Europe.

Before: the accommodation selection flow

  • The flow consisted of three steps: package selection, date selection, and accommodation selection.
  • Package selection occurred on a separate page. Users were required to select a package before being redirected to the accommodation selection page
  • The cheapest accommodation was preselected to promote low-price offers and allow users to quickly proceed to booking without actively comparing options.
  • However, differences between accommodations and their value propositions were not immediately clear. Users had to click “View info” to access detailed information about each option.

Research

A combination of qualitative and quantitative research methods was used to identify usability issues and opportunities for improvement:
  • Eye-tracking user research by an external agency, Unravel.
  • User interviews conducted internally with both returning and first-time users.
  • Brainstorming sessions and exploratory analysis by the design team.
  • Session recordings and analytics (Microsoft Clarity, GA4) to observe real user behavior.

I collaborated with the design team to define interview questions, analyze user sessions, and translate findings into actionable insights.

Insights

Key findings from the research included:
  • Users often skipped or rushed through the accommodation selection step.
  • Users tended to default to the cheapest option without exploring alternatives.
  • Users frequently navigated back and forth between package and accommodation pages when comparing options.

Approach

This project required close collaboration with CRO experts and developers. The work was structured into scoped, testable increments and validated through A/B testing. We broke down this problem into 3 key areas: Booking flow structure (steppers), package selection and the accommodation facilities visibility.

Goal: Optimising the accommodation selection flow

  1. Introduce a stepper component to guide users through the flow

    Provide a clear visual representation of all steps required to complete the booking process.

  2. Reduce back-and-forth navigation

    Minimize disruption by keeping users within a single, continuous flow.

  3. Highlight unique selling points (USPs) of each accommodation

    Ensure key differences between options are visible at a glance, including imagery and facilities.

  4. UX & performance metrics

    Increase conversion rates and user engagement with accommodation options.

Design strategy

  1. Clarity

    Instead of relying on aggressive promotional tactics or forcing progression through the flow, we focused on improving clarity. By making the booking process and accommodation differences more transparent, we aimed to support informed decision-making.

  2. Data-driven decisions

    Design decisions were informed by:
    • Survey data
    • Internal analytics on preferred facilities by our users
    • Public research on user decision-making behavior on hotel bookings.
    • Competitor analysis
    • Behavioral data from analytics and heatmaps
    My main focus was on the companies internal data and user monitoring.

  3. Accessibility

    Accessibility was integrated into each component from the start:

    • Reduced cognitive load through progressive disclosure (steppers).
    • Use of visual cues such as icons.
    • WCAG-compliant color contrast.

Implementation

  • Mobile first

    The design process started with mobile constraints in mind. Given the density of information in OTA platforms, this ensured usability across smaller screens. This approach influenced decisions such as using a select component for package selection.

  • Package selector

    A simplified select component was introduced to display available packages based on the user’s selected departure date, separating available and unavailable options.

  • Accommodation USPs

    Key information such as room size and guest capacity was consistently displayed across all accommodations.

  • Visual design

    Facility categories were represented using icons to improve scannability and draw user attention.

Final design

Steppers

The final design introduced a simple yet visually clear stepper component. Insights from user interviews and behavioral data showed that users tend to scroll quickly—especially on desktop. The stepper provided persistent context, helping users understand their progress and reducing the likelihood of skipping steps.

Package selector

To reduce back-and-forth navigation between package and accommodation pages, we introduced a select component displaying all available packages with the selected hotel. This allowed users to compare and switch options without leaving the flow.

List of accommodation facilities

Displaying key facilities at a glance increased user curiosity and engagement with accommodation options. We observed an increase in interaction with accommodation elements in GA4. Additionally, CRO stakeholders reported improved sales of premium rooms, although exact figures were not made available.
